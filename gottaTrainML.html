the art of war¶

The person who throws the first punch wins a fight 90% of the time. It gives you the advantage in the fight.
The Internet.

Does it really? To do this we need to go back and partition the frequency of wins when it fought first or second.

Here I'm going to test a different approach and later go back to counting by position to see if this approach is good enough. I am "correcting" the fraction of wins by the quotient of $\frac{Second}{First}$. If a fighter fought more frequently as second, the quotient will be $\gt{1}$ and inflate the wins fraction.

In [110]:
FrequencyCombats["WinFraction"]=FrequencyCombats.WinsFreq/FrequencyCombats.TotalFights

Predicting outcomes of fights¶

How can we make use of our data to predict the winner of a fight?

Essentialy what we're going to do is pass some data to a machine learning model (function), evaluate and compare against a known output (winner of the fight). With each observation, the model gets refined so that it will hopefull predict given a list of data who is more likely to win that fight.

What combination of data can we feed the model?

The simples idea I can think of is to just concatenate the list of values for each of the two Pokemon in a combat and evaluate the result as binary. If the first Pokemon wins the output should be 1, else is 0.

We could also pass the difference of the values: $First-Second$
or the ratio $\frac{First}{Second}$

Let's do the simples thing first.
